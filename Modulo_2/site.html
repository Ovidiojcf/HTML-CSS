About Guaraná

The Legend of Guaraná

According to an indigenous legend, a benevolent deity blessed a village with a special child. However, an envious spirit, jealous of his goodness, took his life. In sorrow, the villagers buried the child, and from his eyes, the guaraná plant grew. Its seeds, resembling eyes, became a sacred gift, granting strength and vitality to those who consumed it.

Curiosities & Culture of Guaraná

Guaraná, a plant native to the Amazon, is known for its energizing properties, containing natural caffeine and other stimulating compounds. It has been used for centuries by indigenous tribes and is now a key ingredient in Brazilian drinks and cosmetics. Beyond its practical uses, guaraná is a cultural symbol of vitality and pride, celebrated in various regional festivals.

Guaraná Production and Export in Brazil

Guaraná is primarily produced in the Amazon region of Brazil, where it thrives in the rich soil and humid climate. The seeds are harvested and processed into various products, including powders, extracts, and beverages. Brazil is the world's largest producer and exporter of guaraná, with its products being shipped worldwide. The demand for guaraná has grown significantly due to its use in energy drinks, supplements, and cosmetics, making it a key agricultural export for the country.
